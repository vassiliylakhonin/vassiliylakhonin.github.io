Article Brief: Data Overload in Fitness Tracking Technologies

DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.17215832

Plain-language summary

This article focuses on data overload challenges in fitness-tracking environments, where users and programs often face more metrics than can be meaningfully interpreted. The topic highlights the importance of selecting the right indicators, prioritizing actionable insights, and structuring measurement systems so data supports decisions rather than creating noise.

When this article is relevant

  • KPI design and metric prioritization
  • Dashboard and reporting simplification
  • Data quality vs. data quantity tradeoffs
  • Human-centered interpretation of performance data

Key terms

data overload, fitness tracking, KPI selection, dashboard design, measurement governance, actionable insights
